## Ends and Coends

<https://mathoverflow.net/questions/78471/intuition-for-coends>

::: {.definition .proofenv title="Ends and Coends"}
Definitions:

-   End of a functor $F: \mathsf{C}^{\operatorname{op}}\times \mathsf{C} \to X$: an equalizer `
    <span class="math display">
    \begin{align*}
    \int_{\mathsf{C}} F \coloneqq\int_{x} F(x, x) \rightarrow \prod_{x \in \mathsf{C}} F(x, x) \rightrightarrows \prod_{\mathsf{C}(x, y) } F\left(x, y\right)
    .\end{align*}
    <span>`{=html}

-   Coend: a coequalizer `
    <span class="math display">
    \begin{align*}
    \int^{\mathsf{C}} F \coloneqq\int^{y} F(y, y) \leftarrow \coprod_{y \in \mathsf{C}} F(y, y) \leftleftarrows \coprod_{\mathsf{C}(x, y) } F\left(y, x \right)
    .\end{align*}
    <span>`{=html}
:::

::: {.example .proofenv title="?"}
Examples of (co)ends

-   Can realize global sections: `
    <span class="math display">
    \begin{align*}
    {{\Gamma}\qty{X; {\mathcal{F}}} } = \int_{U \in {\mathsf{Open}}(X)^{\operatorname{op}}} {\mathcal{F}}(U)
    .\end{align*}
    <span>`{=html}

-   Can realize natural transformations as ends: `
    <span class="math display">
    \begin{align*}
    \mathop{\mathrm{Mor}}_{{\mathsf{Fun}}}(F, G) = \int_c \mathsf{C}(F(c), G(c))
    ,\end{align*}
    <span>`{=html} realizing them as a coherent family of morphisms.
:::

-   Idea: given the singular set functor $S({-}): {\mathsf{Top}}\to{\mathsf{sSet}}$ where $S({-})([n]) = {\mathsf{Top}}(\Delta^n, {-})$, construct a left adjoint $L$ (geometric realization). This should give a bijection `
    <span class="math display">
    \begin{align*}
    {\mathsf{Top}}(LX, Y) { { \, \xrightarrow{\sim}\, }}{\mathsf{sSet}}(X, S(Y))
    ,\end{align*}
    <span>`{=html} where homs on the right-hand side are natural transformations.

-   Do this by bending natural transformations: `
    <span class="math display">
    \begin{align*}
    X([n]) \to {\mathsf{Top}}(\Delta^n, Y) \leadsto {\mathsf{Top}}(X[n] \times \Delta^n, R(X))
    ,\end{align*}
    <span>`{=html} where for every map on the right-hand side there is a map $R(X)\to Y$ making a diagram commute. The solution: a coend `
    <span class="math display">
    \begin{align*}
    R(X) \coloneqq\int^n X([n]) \times \Delta^n
    .\end{align*}
    <span>`{=html}

-   Think of functors like modules and coends like tensor products.

-   Think of ends as generalizations of limits to profunctors.

    -   Need to replace cones of functors with *wedges* of profunctors.
